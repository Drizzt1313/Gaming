70's & 80's

Gaming through the ages

The 70's and 80's were the birth decades for gaming. The first consoles were the Atari 2600 and The Magnavox Odyssey. Arcade games were also a giant success, sparking lifelong interest in the gaming world. This was only the beginning, with games like computer space, and pong.

The Magnavox Odyssey was released in 1972 and came with only a few games prebuilt into the system. Televisions were still relatively small, and the picture quality was still advancing. This made it easy for systems like The Magnavox and Atari to take off.

Some of the first arcade games to really make an impact were brought about in the 80’s. Games like Pac Man, Centipede, Galaga, and many others. Gaming at home was interesting, new and fun, but the experience of an arcade was like nothing people had ever known before. Challenges were made, money won and lost, pride broken, and quarters gobbled by flashing machines.

Jumping ahead in time to the mid 80’s the NES and Saga Genisis came to light with games that to this day still rule the virtual world. Games like Super Mario, Sonic the Hedge hog and Zelda. These games made such a profonde impact that they still are some of the most bought games today.

There were many off shoots of these systems, like Saga CD, Nintendo Gameboy one of the first hand held gaming devices, and more. Games will always come and go but the classics will always remain.
